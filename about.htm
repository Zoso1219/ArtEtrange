About the Artist

Life and Career

Born in Toulouse and received a Diplôme national supérieur d'expression plastique from the Ecole Pilote Internationale d'Arte et de Recherche. I lived in Paris and taught at the École supérieure d'art et design Le Havre-Rouen. I have produced watercolors and drawings, as well as murals, sculptures, and photographs. I have had solo shows at the Centre d'Art Neuchâtel and at galleries in Vence, Nice, Paris and Marseille, and have participated in group exhibitions at the Yerba Buena Center for the Arts, at the Musée des Beaux-Arts de Tourcoing, at the Musée d'art contemporain de Lyon, at the BAWAG Foundation in Vienna, at La Criée in Rennes and at the Venice Biennale in 2003.

In 2004, I was one of the fourteen "founder and featured" artists in The Stuckists Punk Victorian held at the Walker Art Gallery for the Liverpool Biennial. I promoted the second Paris Stuckist show in 2005.

In 2005, I illustrated a children's book. In 2006, the Tarot Museum in Bologna acquired a Tarot deck, which I had designed in 1999.

Art

I chose my themes of myths, legends and fairytales as I find reality terribly sad and cruel. I need to escape to another dimension. My style has been likened to Chagall's. A large body of my paintings are of Greek mythology, composing a mass of figurative detail. I respect the force of nature.

Research for a painting is a combination of academic books on the subject and infant picture books, which leads to a few weeks' mental preparation for the main elements. The first part of the painting is the central character, around which other images evolve in an imaginative process to discover images like you might discover figures in a cloud. It takes me three months to complete the painting.
